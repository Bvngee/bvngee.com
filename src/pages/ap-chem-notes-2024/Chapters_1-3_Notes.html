Chapter 1

Scientific Method:

  1. Making observations.

  2. Formulating hypotheses (possible explanations for the observations).

  3. Performing experiments to test hypotheses.

Law: summary of observed measurable behavior. Summarizes what happens.

Theory: An attempt to explain why it happens.

Fundamental SI Units:

physical quantity unit abbreviation
mass kilogram kg
length meter m
time second s
temperature Kelvin K
amount of substance mole mol
electric current ampere A
luminous intensity candela cd

Every measurement has some degree of uncertainty. This is denoted by the use of significant figures. For example, the measurement 1.86kg usually assumes an error of +- 0.01kg.

For multiplication/division, the result has the same # of significant figures as the least precise measurement used in the calculation. For addition/subtraction, the result has the same # of decimal places as the least precise measurement used in the calculation.

Temperature conversions:

F = C*(9/5) + 32 or C = (5/9)*(F - 32)

K = C + 273.15

Density:

Density = Mass / Volume

Classifications of Mixtures:

Homogeneous: Solutions, having visibly indistinguishable parts.

Heterogeneous: Can usually be separated into two or more homogeneous mixtures or pure substances (for example, ice cubes in tea).

Physical changes: a change in the form of a substance, not in its chemical composition. Can be used to separate a mixture into compounds, but it will not break compounds into elements.

Chemical changes: a change in which a given substance becomes a new substance or substances with different properties and different composition.

Compound: a substance with constant composition that can be broken down into elements by chemical processes. Composed of atoms from more than one element held together by chemical bonds.

Element: substances that cannot be decomposed into simpler substances by chemical or physical means.

Chapter 2

Law of conservation of mass: “Mass is neither created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction.”

Law of definite proportion: “A given compound always contains exactly the same proportion of elements by mass.”

(e.x. The substance copper carbonate is always 5.3 parts copper to 4 parts oxygen to 1 part carbon)

Law of multiple proportions: “When two elements form a series of compounds, the ratios of the masses of the second element that combine with 1g of the first element can always be reduced to small whole numbers.”

(e.x. In the compounds CO and CO2, the O has ratios of mass to the mass of carbon that are whole numbers)

Isotopes: atoms with the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons.

2311Na

23 is the Mass number, the total number of protons and neutrons.

11 is the Atomic number, the total number of protons.

Na is the element symbol for sodium.

The forces that hold atoms together in compounds are called chemical bonds.

Covalent bonds: sharing electrons.

Ion: an atom or group of atoms that has a net positive or negative charge.

Cation: Positively charged ion.

Anion: Negatively charged ion.

Since Anions and Cations have opposite charges, they attract each other. This form of attraction is called ionic bonding.

Periodic table (for reference)

Most of the elements are metals. The few nonmetals are in the top right corner. Metals tend to lose electrons to form positive ions, while nonmetals tend to gain electrons to form negative ions.

Vertical columns = groups

Horizontal rows = periods

Elements in the same groups have similar chemical properties (e.x. The alkali metals are very active elements that readily form ions with a 1+ charge when they react with nonmetals).

An Acid is a substance that certain molecules produce when dissolved in water, containing free H+ ions (protons).

Chapter 3

Chemical stoichiometry: The study of quantities of materials consumed and produced in chemical reactions.

Because atoms are so small, we deal with samples of matter that contain huge numbers of atoms. We determine the number of atoms in a given sample by finding its mass, and using the average mass of the atoms.

The modern system of atomic masses is based on 12C (carbon twelve) as the standard, where 12C is assigned exactly 12 atomic mass units (u), which all other atoms’ masses are based on.

Average atomic mass of an element = average atomic mass of the element as it appears in nature (ex: for every 100 atoms of copper, 69.09 are 63Cu and 30.91 are 65Cu, which can be used to calculate average atomic mass (or simply the atomic mass) of copper as shown on periodic table)

A mole is defined as the number of carbon atoms in exactly 12g of pure 12C.

1 mol = 6.022 * 1023 (Avogadro’s number), so

1 mol of a methane = 6.022 * 1023 CH4 molecules.

Ex: 1 mol of Si contains 28.09g of Si, which is its atomic mass.

Molar mass of a substance is the mass in grams of 1 mol of the compound.

The mass percent of an element within a compound can be calculated by finding the mass (in grams) of that element in 1 mole of the compound and comparing it to the total mass (in grams) in 1 mole of the compound.

Chemical reactions are represented by chemical equations with reactants and products. All atoms present in the reactants must be accounted for among the products.

The formulas of compounds in chemical equations must never be changed when balancing the equations.

To determine how much product can be formed from a given mixture of reactants, you look for the reactant that is limiting (the one that runs out first and thus limits the amount of product that can form). This reactant is called the limiting reactant.

SUPER GIGA EXPLANATION OF AMU VS G/MOL:

Amu is a mass unit, and so is the gram. Mole is a quantity. Molar mass represents the number of grams in one mole of a substance. The role of the quantity “mole” (avogadro's number) here is to convert between the different mass units!!! Essentially, avogadro's number can be used to relate grams to amu. “One gram is equivalent to 6.022 x 1023 AMU.”

So when you look at the periodic table and see that Iron (Fe) has a mass number 55.85, that means TWO things. That means that the mass of one atom of Iron is equal to 55.85 amu. But it ALSO means that the mass of one MOLE of Iron is equal to 55.85 grams!!
